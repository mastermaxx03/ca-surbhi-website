## What

The Government of India has notified the **Baggage Rules, 2026**, revising duty-free allowances for passengers arriving in India.  

The updated rules:

- Increase the general duty-free allowance  
- Clarify limits on gold jewellery  
- Allow **one new laptop or notepad duty-free** for eligible passengers  

These rules replace the earlier **Baggage Rules, 2016**.

---

## Why

The changes were introduced to:

- Update outdated baggage limits set nearly a decade ago  
- Adjust allowances in line with rising travel costs and inflation  
- Make international travel more convenient while preventing misuse for commercial purposes  

The focus remains on allowing **personal-use items**, not goods intended for sale.

---

## Who

The revised duty-free benefits apply to:

- Indian residents  
- Tourists of Indian origin  
- Foreign nationals holding a valid visa (other than a tourist visa)  
- Foreign tourists (under a separate allowance)

---

## Gold Jewellery Allowance (Unchanged)

For Indian residents or persons of Indian origin living abroad for more than one year:

- **Female passengers:** up to **40 grams** of gold jewellery  
- **Male passengers:** up to **20 grams** of gold jewellery  

> The jewellery must be part of genuine personal baggage and not intended for resale.

---

## Where

These baggage rules apply to passengers arriving in India by **air or sea** and are enforced by Indian customs authorities at international airports and seaports.

---

## When

The **Baggage Rules, 2026** came into force on **February 2, 2026**.

---

# What Is the General Duty-Free Allowance?

## Meaning

The **General Duty-Free Allowance (GDFA)** is the maximum total value of goods a passenger can bring into India without paying customs duty, provided:

- The goods are for personal use  
- They are carried in accompanied baggage  

It represents the **overall value cap**, not a per-item limit.

---

## Items Covered

The allowance generally includes:

- Clothing and footwear  
- Watches and personal accessories  
- Small electronics and gadgets  
- Gifts and personal effects  

---

## Items Excluded

Certain items are governed by separate rules and do not fall under the general allowance:

- Gold and silver (including jewellery)  
- Alcohol and tobacco  
- Currency  
- One laptop or notepad (allowed separately under new rules)

---

# Old vs New Limits: What Has Changed?

## Comparison: Baggage Rules 2016 vs 2026

| Category | Old Rules (2016) | New Rules (2026) |
|-----------|------------------|------------------|
| General duty-free allowance (Indian residents & eligible passengers) | ₹50,000 | ₹75,000 |
| Foreign tourists duty-free allowance | ₹15,000 | ₹25,000 |
| Gold jewellery – Female passenger | 40 grams | 40 grams |
| Gold jewellery – Male passenger | 20 grams | 20 grams |
| Laptop / notepad allowance | Not clearly specified | 1 new laptop/notepad duty-free (18+) |
| Pooling of allowances | Not allowed | Not allowed |

---

## Additional Key Points

- Duty-free allowances are **per passenger** and cannot be combined or shared  
- If the value of goods exceeds the allowed limit, customs duty is payable **only on the excess**  
- Currency import/export rules continue to be governed by **FEMA (Export and Import of Currency) Regulations, 2015**

---

# Bottom Line

While the **gold jewellery weight limits remain unchanged**, the increase in the general duty-free allowance to **₹75,000** and the explicit duty-free laptop provision make the **Baggage Rules, 2026** more traveller-friendly — especially for Indians returning from abroad.
